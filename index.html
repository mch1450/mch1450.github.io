MSU Parking Finder

Project Overview

MSU Parking Finder is a mobile application that allows users to efficiently find open parking on MSU campus via proximity to destination, neighborhood selection, or through the aid of AR.

Problem Statement

Parking on campus has been a challenging experience for many students at MSU due to the limited amount of space, expensive parking tickets, and difficulty of finding open parking spots quickly during busy times of the day.

Possible Solution

Develop a parking application that will improve the parking experience on campus by giving users ways to quickly find available parking and information about prices and ticket costs.

Qualitative Research

Target User

MSU student or alumni with experience or desire to park on campus

Interview Process

Five participants matching the target user description were selected for a 20 minute interview regarding their experiences parking on MSU campus. The interview aimed to gain insight on student parking behavior, possible concerns regarding the way parking is structured and enforced, and potentional solutions to improve the experience.

Notable Questions & Key Findings

Have you ever gotten a parking ticket?

Every single participant has gotten at least one parking ticket during their time at MSU. Many expressed frustrations with how expensive they have gotten. Violations students received include parking in campus lots without proper passes and not paying for pay-by-plate meters.

What is the hardest part about MSU parking?

Participants mentioned difficulty in finding open spots to park for class. One participant reported taking up to 30 minutes to look for parking during popular time slots. Some participants risk parking tickets when it takes too long to make it for class.

Low Fidelity prototypes

Early concepts include a paper prototype of a mobile app and a pair of cardboard AR glasses that would allow users to find parking within their surroundings in real time.

High Fidelity Prototype Process

After evaluating the paper prototype on 5 MSU students by having them think aloud as they find and copy the address of the closest open parking lot to Wells Hall, new goals and features were planned for the next prototype.

  • Incorporate the AR glasses prototype into the mobile app → AR Mode
  • Incorporate more ways to find open parking → Search around destination feature, Search by neighborhood feature, AR Mode
  • Make it simpler to locate open parking so users don't waste time going to full lots → Color-coded open/full tags for lots, Live updates of parking lot capacities
  • Allow users to quickly access parking lots they like: Bookmark feature, Recent searches feature

AR Mode

In AR mode, users can use their phone camera to scan their surroundings. Nearby lots will pop up with live updates as users move towards or away from their location. Distance can also be adjusted via the slider.
